About

Who I am as an engineer

I like living where mechanics, electronics, and code overlap. Whether it’s a conveyor-based sorting system, a laser weed-removal cart, or a 3D printer playing tic-tac-toe, I enjoy taking a project from idea to working hardware—then tightening up the controls until it behaves.


At MTSU, I’ve spent a lot of time in labs and shops: wiring panels, tuning controllers, writing PLC logic, and helping other students debug their systems. I enjoy figuring out why something doesn’t work, documenting the fix, and making it easy for the next person to repeat.
