Projects

Building real systems, not just simulations

Laser Weed-Removal Cart (60W CO₂ + Steering Optics)

Motion · Safety · Optics
SolidWorks & machining Motor control & interlocks Optics & laser steering

A mobile platform designed to burn weeds using a 60W laser, with a mirror-based steering assembly to position the beam and integrated motion and safety interlocks.

  • Co-led the design and build of the cart structure, drive system, and laser mounting, from SolidWorks CAD to machined aluminum parts.
  • Implemented steering optics using high-speed motors and mirrors to direct the beam to the target area for efficient weed burning.
  • Focused on safe power distribution, fusing, and E-stop behavior for laser and motion hardware.

Automated Box & Can Sorting System (Cognex + PLC)

Conveyors · Pneumatics · Machine Vision
Rockwell / PLC Cognex In-Sight Photoeyes & encoders

A conveyor-based sorter that takes a mixed stream of boxes and cans from a hopper, detects each item with a Cognex camera, and kicks it into the correct bin using a PLC-controlled pneumatic cylinder.

  • Designed and tuned the hopper and conveyor system to maximize singulation and reduce double-feeds into the vision zone.
  • Integrated Cognex In-Sight with the PLC, setting up inspections, digital outputs, and I/O mapping for sort decisions.
  • Developed test procedures to measure throughput and sorting accuracy, logging data for different speeds and hopper load levels.

Tic-Tac-Toe Robot on a 3D Printer (MATLAB + G-code)

Motion Control · MATLAB · G-code
Ender 3D Printer Pen plotter Game logic

Converted an Ender 3D Printer into a tic-tac-toe robot by mounting a pen, generating G-code toolpaths from MATLAB, and adding basic game logic so it can play against a human on a 3×3 grid.

  • Wrote MATLAB scripts to translate board state into coordinates and generate clean G-code moves for X/Y/Z.
  • Calibrated steps/mm, homing offsets, and soft limits to get consistent, repeatable lines and centered symbols on each square.
  • Debugged motion issues like backlash and missed steps, and tuned speeds and accelerations for reliable plotting.

Custom Bluetooth Speaker with Op-Amp Audio Stage

Analog Electronics · Audio · Enclosure Design
Op-amp amplifier Bluetooth module Power & wiring

Built a Bluetooth speaker from scratch, combining a Bluetooth audio module with a custom op-amp-based amplifier stage, power regulation, and a hand-built enclosure.

  • Designed and tested the op-amp circuit to get clean gain and low noise while driving the speaker load from a battery-powered supply.
  • Integrated the Bluetooth module, amplifier, and power circuitry with proper grounding and wiring layout to minimize hum and interference.
  • Prototyped the enclosure, mounting hardware, and controls to make the system durable and easy to use.

ESP32 Temperature-Controlled Fan with Occupancy Sensing

Embedded · PID Control · Prototyping
ESP32 PID speed control IR presence sensor

A proof-of-concept smart fan that uses an ESP32 to regulate fan speed based on room temperature, but only runs when an infrared sensor detects a person in the room.

  • Read temperature and used a PID loop to smoothly control the fan speed instead of simple on/off thresholds, reducing overshoot and oscillation.
  • Gated fan operation with an infrared presence sensor so the system only consumes power when a human is detected in the space.
  • Drove a small DC fan as a proof-of-concept platform for applying embedded control, sensor fusion, and tuning in a compact project.
